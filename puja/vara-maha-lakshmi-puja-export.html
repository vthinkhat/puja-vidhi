ವರಮಹಾಲಕ್ಷ್ಮಿ ಪೂಜ (Varamahalakshmi Puja)

This puja serves three important purposes: performing the Varamahalakshmi Vratam, Sharava Sukravaram Lakshmi puja, and conducting the daily (Nitya) puja. When done regularly as a daily ritual, it is believed to remove poverty and bring lasting prosperity to the household.

There are no restrictions for performing this puja. For better financial blessings, it is recommended to have the husband present and seated during the ceremony. The puja can be conducted either in the morning (Prabhatha veela) or in the evening (Pradosha veela). On the day of the puja, devotees should observe celibacy, avoid non-vegetarian food, and consume only sattvic (pure, vegetarian) meals.

The Puja consists of five main parts:

Peetam and Kalasham

Set up a peetam by applying rice flour designs (moggu), then add rice grains, then place a clean cloth over it. Prepare the kalasham by filling it with water, adding akshata (rice mixed with turmeric) and coins; decorate with mango leaves and topping with a coconut.

General Puja

Perform Aachamaniyam (purification), Sankalpam (intention), Kalasha Aradhana, and Ganapati Puja. Follow this with the Shodashopachara (16-step) puja and Anga Puja (offering to the body parts of the deity). Chant the Ashtottaram, and if possible Kanakadhara and Sri Suktam. Complete with dhoopa (incense), deepa (lamp), and naivedyam (offering food).

Thooram (Nine Threads)

Bunch nine cotton threads together and knot them nine times. This is called Grathi or Grathu, symbolizing the goddess's favorite number, nine. Recite nine mantras, offering kumkum or akshata nine times to thooram.

Vratha Katha (Story of the Vow)

Recite the story of Chaarumathi, who is believed to have performed this puja for the first time. The story is symbolic and should not be interpreted literally.

Vaayana Pradhaanam (Distribution of Gifts)

Conclude by giving gifts to the women present, symbolizing generosity and goodwill.

udānaṁ namaḥ - samānaṁ namaḥ
