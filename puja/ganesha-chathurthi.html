ಸಂಕಲ್ಪಂ (Sankalpam)

ಮಮ ಉಪಾತ್ತ ಸಮಸ್ತ ದುರಿತಕ್ಷಯ ದ್ವಾರಾ ಶ್ರೀ ವರಸಿದ್ಧಿ ವಿನಾಯಕ ದೇವತಾ ಪ್ರೀತ್ಯರ್ಥಂ
mama upaatta samasta duritakshaya dvaaraa Shree varasiddhi vinaayaka devata preetyardhaM ಶುಭೇ ಶೋಭನೇ ಮುಹೂರ್ತೇ, ಸಮಸ್ತ ದೇವತಾ ಬ್ರಾಹ್ಮಣ ಹರಿಹರ ಗುರುಚರಣ ಸನ್ನಿಧೌ
Subhae SObhanae muhoortae, samasta daevataa braahmaNa harihara guru charaNa sannidhau ಸರವ ವಿಘ್ನ ನಿವಾರಣಾರ್ಧಮ್
sarva vighna nivaaraNaardhaM
ಶ್ರೀ ಶ್ರೀ ವರಸಿದ್ಧಿ ವಿನಾಯಕ ದೇವತಾಂ ಉದ್ದಿಶ್ಯ ಯಾವಚ್ಛಕ್ತಿ ಧ್ಯಾನ ಆವಾಹನಾದಿ ಷೋಡಶೋಪಚಾರ ಪೂಜಾಂ ಕರಿಷ್ಯೇ
Shree varasiddhi vinaayaka daevataaM uddiSya yaavaChchakti dhyaana aavaahanaadi shodashopacharaaM poojaaM karishyae

ಗಣಪತಿ ಪೂಜೆ(Ganapati Puja)

ಆದೌ ನಿರ್ವಿಘ್ನ-ಪರಿ-ಸಮಾಪ್ತ-ಯರ್ಥಂ ಶ್ರೀ ಗಣಾಧಿಪತಿ ಪ್ರಾರ್ಥನಾಂ ಕರಿಷ್ಯೇ ॥
Ādau nirvighna-pari-samāpti-artham Sree gaNaadhipati praardhanaaM karishyae ||

ವಕ್ರತುಂಡ ಮಹಾಕಾಯ ಸೂರ್ಯಕೋಟಿ ಸಮಪ್ರಭ ।
ನಿರ್ವಿಘ್ನಂ ಕುರು ಮೇ ದೇವ ಸರ್ವಕಾರ್ಯೇಷು ಸರ್ವದಾ ॥
Vakratunda Mahakaya Suryakoti Samaprabha
Nirvighnam Kuru Me Deva Sarvakaryeshu Sarvada

ಸುಮುಖಶ್ಚ ಏಕದಂತಶ್ಚ ಕಪಿಲೋ ಗಜಕರ್ಣಕಃ ।
ಲಂಬೋದರಶ್ಚ ವಿಕಟೋ ವಿಘ್ನರಾಜೋ ಗಣಾಧಿಪಃ ॥
ಧೂಮ್ರಕೇತುರ್ಗಣಾಧ್ಯಕ್ಷೋ ಫಲಚಂದ್ರೋ ಗಜಾನನಃ ।
ವಕ್ರತುಂಡೋ ಶೂರ್ಪಕರ್ಣೋ ಹೇರಂಭಃ ಸ್ಕಂದಪೂರ್ವಜಃ ॥
ಷೋಡಶೈತಾನಿ ನಾಮಾನಿ ಯಃ ಪಠೇದ್ಛೃಣುಯಾದಪಿ ।
ವಿದ್ಯಾರಂಭೇ ವಿವಾಹೇ ಚ ಪ್ರವೇಶೇ ನಿರ್ಗಮೇ ತಥಾ ।
ಸಂಗ್ರಾಮೇ ಸರ್ವಕಾರ್ಯೇಷು ವಿಘ್ನಸ್ತಸ್ಯ ನ ಜಾಯತೇ ॥
Sumukhascha Ekadantascha Kapilo Gajakarṇakah
Lambodarascha Vikato Vighnarajo Ganadhipah
Dhumraketur Ganadhyaksho Phalachandro Gajananah
Vakratundo Shurpakarno Herambhah Skandapurvajah
Shodashaitani Namani Yah Pathed Shrunuyadapi
Vidyarambhe Vivaahe Cha Praveshe Nirgame Tatha
Sangrame Sarvakaryeshu Vighnas Tasya Na Jayate

ಧ್ಯಾನ (dhyaanaM)

ಭವಸಂಚಿತ ಪಾಪೌಘ ವಿಧ್ವಂಸನ ವಿಚಕ್ಷಣಂ
ವಿಘ್ನಾಂಧಕಾರ ಭಾಸ್ವಂತಂ ವಿಘ್ನರಾಜ ಮಹಂಭಜೇ
ಏಕದಂತಂ ಶೂರ್ಪಕರ್ಣಂ ಗಜವಕ್ತ್ರಂ ಚತುರ್ಭುಜಂ
ಪಾಶಾಂಕುಶ ಧರಂ ದೇವಂ ಧ್ಯಾಯೇ ತ್ಸಿದ್ಧಿವಿನಾಯಕಂ
ಧ್ಯಾಯೇದ್ಗಜಾನನಂ ದೇವಂ ತಪ್ತಕಾಂಚನ ಸನ್ನಿಭಂ
ಚತುರ್ಭುಜಂ ಮಹಾಕಾಯಂ ಸರ್ವಾಭರಣಭೂಷಿತಂ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಧ್ಯಾಯಾಮಿ
bhavasaMchita paapaugha vidhvaMsana vichakshaNaM
vighnaaMdhakaara bhaasvaMtaM vighnaraaja mahaMbhajae
aekadaMtaM SoorpakarNaM gajavaktraM chaturbhujaM
paaSaaMkuSa dharaM daevaM dhyaayae tsiddhivinaayakaM
dhaayaedgajaananaM daevaM taptakaaMchana sannibhaM
chaturbhujaM mahaakaayaM sarvaabharaNabhooshitaM
Sree mahaagaNaadhipatayae namaḥ dhyayaami

ಆವಾಹನಂ (Avahanam)

ಅತ್ರಾಗಛ ಜಗದ್ವಂದ್ಯ ಸುರರಾಜಾರ್ಚಿತೇಶ್ವರ
ಅನಾಥನಾಥ ಸರ್ವಜ್ಞ ಗೌರೀಗರ್ಭ ಸಮುದ್ಭವ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಆವಹಯಾಮಿ
Atragachcha jagadvandya surarajaarchiteshwara
Anaathanatha sarvagnaa gaureegarbhha samudbhhava
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah aavahayami

ಆಸನಂ (Asanam)

ಮೌಕ್ತಿಕೈಃ ಪುಷ್ಯರಾಗೈಶ್ಚ ನಾನಾರತ್ನೆರ್ವಿರಾಜಿತಂ
ರತ್ನ ಸಿಂಹಾಸನಂ ಚಾರು ಪ್ರೀತ್ಯರ್ಥಂ ಪ್ರತಿಗೃಹ್ಯತಾಂ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಆಸನಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Mauktikaih pushyaraagaishcha naanaratnerviraajitam
Ratna simhaasanam chaaru preetyartham pratigrihyataam
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah aasanam samarpayaami

ಪಾದ್ಯಂ (Paadyam)

ಗಜವಕ್ತ್ರ ನಮಸ್ತೇಸ್ತು ಸರ್ವಾಭೀಷ್ಟಪ್ರದಾಯಕ
ಭಕ್ತ್ಯಾ ಪಾದ್ಯಂ ಮಯಾದತ್ತಂ ಗೃಹಾಣ ದ್ವಿರದಾನನ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಪಾದ್ಯಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Gajavaktra namastestu sarvaabheeshtapradaayaka
Bhaktyaa paadyam mayaadattam grihaana dviradaanana
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah paadyam samarpayaami

ಆರ್ಘ್ಯಂ (Arghyam)

ಗೌರೀಪುತ್ರ ನಮಸ್ತೇಸ್ತು ಶಂಕರಪ್ರಿಯನಂದನ
ಗೃಹಾಣಾರ್ಘ್ಯಂ ಮಯಾದತ್ತಂ ಗಂಧಪುಷ್ಪಾಕ್ಷತೈರ್ಯುತಂ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಆರ್ಘ್ಯಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Gauriputra namastestu Shankara priya nandana
Grihanarghyam maya dattam gandha pushpakshatairyutam
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah arghyam samarpayaami

ಆಚಮನೀಯಂ (Achamaniyam)

ಅನಾಥನಾಥ ಸರ್ವಜ್ಞ ಗೀರ್ವಾಣ ಗಣ ಪೂಜಿತ
ಗೃಹಾಣಾಚಮನಂ ದೇವ ತುಭ್ಯಂ ದತ್ತಂ ಮಯಾ ಪ್ರಭೋ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಆಚಮನೀಯಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Anathanatha sarvagna Girvana gana pujitah
Grihanachamanam deva tubhyam dattam maya prabho
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah achamaniyam samarpayaami

ಮಧುಪರ್ಕಂ (Madhuparkam)

ದಧಿಕ್ಷೀರ ಸಮಾಯುಕ್ತಂ ಮಥ್ವಾಜ್ಯೇನ ಸಮನ್ವಿತಂ
ಮಧುಪರ್ಕಂ ಗೃಹಾಣೇದಂ ಗಜವಕ್ತ್ರ ನಮೋಸ್ತುತೆ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಮಧುಪರ್ಕಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Dadhi ksheera samayuktam mathvajyena samanvitam
Madhuparkam grihanedam Gajavaktra namostute
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah madhuparkam samarpayaami

ಸ್ನಾನಂ (Snanam)

ಸ್ನಾನಂ ಪಂಚಾಮೃತೈದೇರ್ವ ಗೃಹಾಣ ಗಣನಾಯಕ
ಅನಾಥನಾಥ ಸರ್ವಜ್ಞ ಗೀರ್ವಾಣ ಗಣಪೂಜಿತ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಪಂಚಾಮೃತಸ್ನಾನಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
ಗಂಗಾದಿಸರ್ವತೀರ್ಥೇಭ್ಯ ಆಹೃತೈರ್ಮಲೈರ್ಜಲೈಃ
ಸ್ನಾನಂ ಕುರುಷ್ವ ಭಗವನ್ ಉಮಾಪುತ್ರ ನಮೋಸ್ತುತೆ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಶುದ್ಧೋದಕಸ್ನಾನಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Snanam panchamritair deva grihana gananayaka
Anathanatha sarvagna Girvana ganapujita
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah panchamrita snanam samarpyami
Gangadi sarvatirthebhya aahrutair malair jalaih
Snanam kurushva Bhagavan Umaaputra namostute
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah shuddhodaka snanam samarpyami

ವಸ್ತ್ರಂ (Vastram)

ರಕ್ತವಸ್ತ್ರದ್ವಯಂ ಚಾರು ದೇವಯೋಗ್ಯಂ ಚ ಮಂಗಳಂ
ಶುಭಪ್ರದಂ ಗೃಹಾಣ ತ್ವಂ ಲಂಬೋದರ ಹರಾತ್ಮಜ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ವಸ್ತ್ರಯುಗ್ಮಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Rakta vastra dvayam charu devayogyam cha mangalam
Shubhapradam grihana tvam Lambodara Haraatmaja
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah vastrayugmam samarpyami

ಯಜ್ಞೋಪವೀತಂ (Yajnopavitam)

ರಾಜಿತಂ ಬ್ರಹ್ಮಸೂತ್ರಂ ಕಾಂಚನಂಚೋತ್ತರೀಯಕಂ
ಗೃಹಾಣ ದೇವ ಸರ್ವಜ್ಞ ಭಕ್ತಾನಾಮಿಷ್ಟದಾಯಕ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಯಜ್ಞೋಪವೀತಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Rajitam brahmasutram kanchanam chottariyakam
Grihana deva sarvagna bhaktaanaam ishtadaayaka
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah yajnopavitam samarpyami

ಗಂಧಂ (gaMdhaM)

ಚಂದನಾಗರುಕರ್ಪೂರ ಕಸ್ತೂರೀ ಕುಂಕುಮಾನ್ವಿತಂ
ವಿಲೇಪನಂ ಸುರಶ್ರೇಷ್ಠ ಪ್ರೀತ್ಯರ್ಥಂ ಪ್ರತಿಗೃಹ್ಯತಾಂ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಗಂಧಂ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Chandanagaru karpoora kasturi kunkumaanvitam
Vilepanam sura shreshtha preetyartham pratigrihyataam
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah gandham samarpyami

ಪುಷ್ಪಾಕ್ಷತಗಳು (Pushpakshatalu)

ಅಕ್ಷತಾನ್ ಧವಳಾನ್ ದಿವ್ಯಾನ್ ಶಾಲಿಯಾಂ ಸ್ತಂಡುಲಾನ್ ಶುಭಾನ್
ಗೃಹಾಣ ಪರಮಾನಂದ ಶಂಭು ಪುತ್ರ ನಮೋಸ್ತುತೆ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಅಕ್ಷತಾನ್ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ

ಸುಗಂಧಾನಿ ಸುಪುಷ್ಪಾಣಿ ಜಾಜಿಕುಂದಮುಖಾನಿಚ
ಏಕವಿಶಂತಿ ಪತ್ರಾಣಿ ಸಂಗೃಹಾಣ ನಮೋಸ್ತುತೆ
ಶ್ರೀ ಮಹಾಗಣಾಧಿಪತಯೇ ನಮಃ ಪುಷ್ಪಾಣಿ ಸಮರ್ಪಯಾಮಿ
Akshataan dhavalaan divyaan shaaliyaam standulaan shubhaan
Grihana paramaananda Shambhu putra namostute
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah akshataan samarpyami

Sugandhaani supushpaani jaaji kunda mukhaani cha
Eka vishanti patraani sangrihana namostute
Sri Mahaganadhipataye namah pushpaani samarpyami

ಅಂಗಪೂಜಾ (Anga Puja)

ಗಣೇಶಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಪಾದೌ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಏಕದಂತಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಜಾನುನಿ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ವಿಘ್ನರಾಜಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಜಂಘೇ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಆಖುವಾಹನಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಊರುಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಹೇರಂಬಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಕಟಿಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಲಂಬೋದರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಉದರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಗಣನಾಥಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಹೃದಯಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಸ್ಥೂಲಕಂಠಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಕಂಠಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಸ್ಕಂದಾಗ್ರಜಾಯ ನಮಃ - ನಾಭಿಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಪಾಶಹಸ್ತಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಹಸ್ತೌ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಗಜವಕ್ತ್ರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ವಕ್ತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ವಿಘ್ನಹಂತ್ರೇ ನಮಃ - ನೇತ್ರೇ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಶೂರ್ಪಕರ್ಣಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಕರ್ಣೌ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಫಾಲಚಂದ್ರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಲಲಾಟಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ಸರ್ವೇಶ್ವರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಶಿರಃ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
ವಿಘ್ನರಾಜಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಸರ್ವಾಣ್ಯಾಂಗಾನಿ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ

ಏಕವಿಂಶತಿಪತ್ರ ಪೂಜಾ (aekaviMSati patra pooja )

1. ಸುಮಖಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಮಾಚೀಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
2. ಗಣಾಧಿಪಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಬೃಹತೀಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
3. ಉಮಾಪುತ್ರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಬಿಲ್ವಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
4. ಗಜಾನನಾಯ ನಮಃ - ದುರ್ವಾಯುಗ್ಮಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
5. ಹರಸೂನವೆಯ ನಮಃ - ದುತ್ತೂರಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
6. ಲಂಬೋದರಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಬದರೀಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
7. ಗುಹಾಗ್ರಜಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಅಪಾಮಾರ್ಗಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
8. ಗಜಕರ್ಣಾಯ ನಮಃ - ತುಲಸೀಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
9. ಏಕದಂತಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಚೂತಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
10. ವಿಕಟಾಯ ನಮಃ - ಕರವೀರಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ
11. ಭಿನ್ನದಂತಾಯ ನಮಃ - ವಿಷ್ಣುಕ್ರಾಂತಪತ್ರಂ ಪೂಜಯಾಮಿ

ವ್ರತ ಕಥೆ (Vrata Katha)

The Story of Lord Vinayaka’s Birth and the Moon’s Curse

In ages long past, the venerable sage Suta spoke to his disciples and recounted a tale most sacred — the origin of Lord Vinayaka, the mystery of why one must not gaze upon the Moon on Vinayaka Chaturthi, and the means by which one may escape the curse of that forbidden glance.

The Tale of Gajasura and Lord Shiva

There once lived a fearsome asura named Gajasura. True to his name, he bore the form of an elephant — immense in strength and unyielding in will. Consumed by the hunger for power, Gajasura undertook terrible austerities, enduring the fiercest penance to win the favor of Lord Shiva.

Shiva, moved by such devotion, appeared before him. “Ask for your boon,” he said.

Without hesitation, Gajasura declared, “O Mahadeva, dwell within my stomach. Let your presence be my greatest treasure.”

Such was Shiva’s compassion for his devotees that he agreed, entering the asura’s body and sealing himself within.

Far away in Kailasa, Parvati sensed the absence of her beloved. Her heart grew restless, her search frantic, until she discovered the unthinkable: Shiva imprisoned in Gajasura’s belly. Fear gave way to desperation, and she turned to her brother Vishnu for help.

“Do not weep, Sister,” Vishnu assured her. “I will bring him back.”

Vishnu’s Clever Plan

Vishnu summoned the gods — Indra, Brahma, and others — and together they devised a stratagem. Nandi, Shiva’s loyal bull, was adorned as a Gangireddu, a richly decorated dancing bull as seen in the villages of Andhra. The gods themselves became wandering musicians, each carrying an instrument, each ready to play their part.

Thus disguised, they entered Gajasura’s capital. A dazzling performance unfolded — Nandi danced with breathtaking grace to divine music, captivating the city. The fame of the spectacle reached Gajasura himself, who demanded the troupe’s presence in his court.

There, the asura was entranced. Nandi’s dance delighted him beyond measure. “Name your boon,” Gajasura proclaimed. “It shall be yours.”

In that moment, Vishnu shed the disguise and revealed their intent. “This bull is Nandi, vahana of Lord Shiva. We have come for him.”

The demon king’s heart sank. He recognized Vishnu — the slayer of asuras — and knew his end was near. Yet bound by honor, he did not resist. Instead, he turned his devotion once more to Shiva:

“O Lord, I ask only this final boon: let my head be worshipped in all the worlds, and may you wear my skin as your raiment.”

Shiva, ever merciful, consented. At Vishnu’s command, Nandi tore open Gajasura’s stomach, and Shiva stepped forth, freed at last.

Vishnu, with gentle firmness, advised, “Boons must be granted with caution, lest demons twist them into instruments of destruction.” Shiva bowed to the wisdom of his brother. Then, mounted upon Nandi, he returned with the gods to Kailasa.

The Birth of Ganesha

At Kailasa, Parvati’s joy knew no bounds. To celebrate her husband’s return, she prepared for a sacred abhyangana snana, an anointing bath. She rubbed oil over her skin, cleansing herself with a soft paste of flour known as nalugu. When she washed it away, she gathered the fragrant mixture, and from it, shaped the form of a radiant young boy. With her divine breath, she gave him life.

“My child,” she said tenderly, “stand guard at the doorway while I bathe. Let none pass without my permission.”

The boy obeyed, standing watch like a steadfast sentinel.

The Conflict with Shiva

Soon, Shiva himself approached, desiring entry. The boy stepped forward, blocking the way.

“No one may pass,” he declared.

Shiva’s brow furrowed. Anger flared. Unaware that the boy was Parvati’s creation, he raised his weapon and, in a single act of wrath, struck him down — severing his head and burning it to ashes. Without another thought, Shiva walked inside.

When Parvati emerged, fresh and radiant, she asked after her son. Shiva told her all.

Her joy dissolved into grief. “That child was born of my hands, given breath by my power,” she cried. Her sorrow turned to fury. “You must restore him — or face my wrath.”

Shiva, pierced with regret, vowed to bring the boy back. “But he requires a new head,” he said. His attendants were dispatched with orders: find the head of the first creature lying with its face turned north.

They searched the worlds, yet only Gajasura, in his final repose, lay with his head to the north. They brought back the elephant’s head.

Thus, Shiva placed it upon the lifeless body, breathed life anew, and Parvati’s son rose again — transformed.

This was Gajanana, the elephant-headed god. To honor him, Shiva bestowed a unique vahana — a humble rat, Anindyudu — and proclaimed him Vinayaka, Ganapati, remover of obstacles and bestower of blessings.

Vighneshwara: The Lord of Obstacles

Years passed. Parvati bore another son — Kumaraswamy, destined to lead the armies of heaven, revered as Kartikeya, Skanda, Subramanya.

One day, gods and sages petitioned Shiva:

“O Lord, whenever men begin a noble task, obstacles assail them before even the first step is taken. Appoint a guardian, a lord of the Vighnas (obstructions).”

Kumaraswamy spoke eagerly: “Father, grant me this charge. I am strong, swift, and worthy.”

But Ganesha too sought the role. To decide fairly, Shiva set a challenge: each must journey across the worlds, bathe in every holy river, and return. The first to arrive would be Vighneshwara.

Kumaraswamy, mounted on his swift peacock, flew away at once.

Ganesha, heavy and slow, stood sorrowful. “How can I compete, Father? My body is stout, my pace sluggish. Show me the way.”

Shiva smiled. “My son, to circumambulate your parents with devotion while chanting the Narayana Mantra is equal to bathing in all sacred rivers.”

Ganesha’s heart leapt. He bowed, chanted, and walked in reverence around Shiva and Parvati. By the power of this act, everywhere Kumaraswamy bathed, he found Ganesha had already been there before him.

At last, humbled, Kumaraswamy returned. “Father,” he said softly, “I see now my brother’s greatness. Appoint him as Vighneshwara.”

Thus, on Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi, Ganesha was crowned the Lord of Obstacles. From that day, the festival of Vinayaka Chaturthi was celebrated with devotion.

The Moon’s Mockery and the Curse

When the feasting ended, Ganesha bent to offer sashtanga namaskaram — full prostration before his parents. Yet his great belly, heavy with sweets, made him falter comically — legs rising as hands fell, hands rising as legs fell.

The Moon, perched in the heavens, beheld the sight and burst into laughter. His mocking gaze, sharp and cruel, cast an evil drishti. In an instant, Ganesha’s stomach split, and he fell lifeless.

Parvati, aghast, cursed the Moon:

“For mocking my son and casting your wicked gaze, hear my words: whosoever looks upon you shall be accused falsely, dishonored, and shamed!”

This became the Neelaapaninda curse.

The gods rushed to revive Ganesha, and Parvati, softened by relief, mitigated her curse. “The curse shall remain only on Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi. On that day alone must men avoid the Moon.”

The Tale of the Syamantaka Mani

In Dwapara Yuga, on such a Chaturthi, Sage Narada visited Krishna in Dwaraka and told him of the curse. Krishna spread the word, careful not to see the Moon. Yet fate played its part — as he milked a cow, he glimpsed the Moon’s reflection in the vessel.

Knowing what must follow, he steeled himself.

Soon Satrajit, a courtier, arrived bearing the dazzling Syamantaka Mani, a jewel gifted by Surya that produced eight kilograms of gold each day. Krishna urged him to dedicate it for the welfare of the kingdom, but Satrajit kept it for himself.

His brother Prasena later wore the jewel on a hunt. A lion slew him, mistaking the gem for flesh, only to be slain by a mighty bear, who gave the jewel to his child as a plaything.

When Prasena never returned, Satrajit accused Krishna of murder and theft — the curse had found its mark.

To prove his innocence, Krishna traced the truth: Prasena’s corpse, the lion’s tracks, the bear’s cave. There, the bear-king Jambavantha attacked, and for twenty-eight days they fought with earth-shaking fury. At last, Jambavantha recognized Krishna as Rama incarnate, his master from Treta Yuga. He fell at Krishna’s feet, offering both the jewel and his daughter Jambavati in marriage.

Krishna accepted her hand, returned the gem, and cleared his name. Ashamed, Satrajit begged forgiveness, giving Krishna both the jewel and his daughter Satyabhama. Krishna returned the jewel but accepted Satyabhama as his bride.

The Remedy for the Curse

The sages asked Krishna: “O Lord, you are divine and could prove your innocence. But what of us, mere mortals? If we see the Moon on Vinayaka Chaturthi, how shall we be spared?”

Krishna, ever merciful, granted: “He who performs Ganesh Puja on this day, listens with devotion to the story of Ganesha’s birth and of the Syamantaka Mani, and finally places akshintalu — grains of rice mixed with turmeric — upon his head, shall be freed from the curse, even if he sees the Moon.”

And so, even today, the faithful recite this sacred story on Vinayaka Chaturthi, remembering the birth of Ganesha, the fall of Gajasura, the laughter of the Moon, and the jewel of Satrajit — and by doing so, they are blessed.
