ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ತಂತ್ರಃ (Vyuha Lakshmi Tantram)

ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ತಂತ್ರಃ (PART 1/3 - 1 time)

ದಯಾಲೋಲ ತರಂಗಾಕ್ಷೀ ಪೂರ್ಣಚಂದ್ರ ನಿಭಾನನಾ ।
Whose eyes is full of compassion; whose face is like full moon
 ಜನನೀ ಸರ್ವಲೋಕಾನಾಂ ಮಹಾಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀಃ ಹರಿಪ್ರಿಯಾ ॥ 1 ॥
who is mother of all worlds; beloved of hari.. goddess mahalakshmi

ಸರ್ವಪಾಪ ಹರಾಸೈವ ಪ್ರಾರಬ್ಧಸ್ಯಾಪಿ ಕರ್ಮಣಃ ।
Indeed you are destroyer of all sins; you even destroy accumulated karma
 ಸಂಹೃತೌ ತು ಕ್ಷಮಾಸೈವ ಸರ್ವ ಸಂಪತ್ಪ್ರದಾಯಿನೀ ॥ 2 ॥
She forgive even at time of destruction; she bestows all wealth & prosperity

ತಸ್ಯಾ ವ್ಯೂಹ ಪ್ರಭೇದಾಸ್ತು ಲಕ್ಷೀಃ ಸರ್ವಪಾಪ ಪ್ರಣಾಶಿನೀ ।
Of her different forms; lakshmi is destroyer if all sins
 ತತ್ರಯಾ ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ಸಾ ಮುಗ್ಧಾಃ ಕಾರುಣ್ಯ ವಿಗ್ರಹ ॥ 3 ॥
The three-fold form (lakshmi, keerthi, jayam) of lakshmi, she is am embodiment of compassionate and gentleness

ಅನಾಯಾಸೇನ ಸಾ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀಃ ಸರ್ವಪಾಪ ಪ್ರಣಾಶಿನೀ ।
Effortless (with utmost ease) she destroys all sins
 ಸರ್ವೈಶ್ವರ್ಯ ಪ್ರದಾ ನಿತ್ಯಂ ತಸ್ಯಾ ಮಂತ್ರಮಿಮಂ ಶೃಣು ॥ 4 ॥
she eternally bestows all forms of wealth & prospertiy; listen this mantra of hers

ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ಮಂತ್ರಃ (ಬೀಜಾಕ್ಷರ ಸಹಿತಂ) (PART 2/3 - 108 times)

ಓಂ ಶ್ರೀ ಓಂ ನಮಃ ॥
ಪರಮಲಕ್ಷ್ಮ್ಮೈ, ವಿಷ್ಣು-ವಕ್ಷಸ್ಥಿತಾಯೈ, ರಮಾಯೈ, ಆಶ್ರಿತ-ತಾರಕಾಯೈ ನಮೋ, ವಹ್ನಿಜಾಯೈ ನಮಃ ॥

ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ಮಂತ್ರಃ (ಸ್ಲೋಕ) (PART 2/3 - 108 times)

ವೇದಾದಿಮಾಯೈ ಮಾತ್ರೇ ಚ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಮ್ಯೈ ನತಿ ಪದಂ ವದೇತ್ ।
ಪರಮೇತಿ ಪದಂ ಚೋಕ್ತ್ರಾ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಮ್ಯಾ ಇತಿ ಪದಂ ತತಃ ॥ 5 ॥

ವಿಷ್ಣು ವಕ್ಷಃ ಸ್ಥಿತಾಯೈ ಸ್ಯಾತ್ ಮಾಯಾ ಶ್ರೀತಾರಿಕಾ ತತಃ ।
ವಹ್ನಿ ಜಾಯಾಂತ ಮಂತ್ರೋಯಂ ಅಭೀಷ್ಟಾರ್ಥ ಸುರದ್ರುಮಃ ॥ 6 ॥

ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷ್ಮೀ ತಂತ್ರಃ (PART 3/3 - 1 time)

ದ್ವಿಭೂಜಾ ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷೀಸ್ಸ್ಯಾತ್ ಬಧ್ಧ ಪದ್ಮಾಸನ ಪ್ರಿಯಾ ।
ಶ್ರೀನಿವಾಸಾಂಗ ಮಧ್ಯಸ್ಥಾ ಸುತರಾಂ ಕೇಶವಪ್ರಿಯಾ ॥ 7 ॥

ತಾಮೇವ ಶರಣಂ ಗಛ್ಛ ಸರ್ವಭಾವೇನ ಸತ್ವರಮ್ ।
ಇತಿ ಮಂತ್ರಂ ಉಪಾದಿಶ್ಯ ದದೃಶೇ ನ ಕುತ್ರಚಿತ್ ॥ 8 ॥

Description in Varaha Purana

The Goddess on the Lord's chest is also known as "Vyuha Lakshmi." The Varaha Purana describes the two-handed Vyuha Lakshmi seated on the Lord's chest in a "baddha padmasana" (lotus) posture in the following verse:

ದ್ವಿಭೂಜಾ ವ್ಯೂಹಲಕ್ಷೀಸ್ಸ್ಯಾತ್, ಬಧ್ಧ ಪದ್ಮಾಸನ ಪ್ರಿಯಾ ।
ಶ್ರೀನಿವಾಸಾಂಗ ಮಧ್ಯಸ್ಥಾ ಸುತರಾಂ ಕೇಶವಪ್ರಿಯಾ ॥ 7 ॥

Sri Ramanuja consecrated the image of Vyuha Lakshmi on Lord Venkateswara's chest on Sukla Dwadasi, Friday, under the Uttara Phalguni star. This auspicious timing was known as "Ratna Manjarika Yoga." This was done to exponentially increase the wealth and prosperity of the Lord. The temple priests had been performing daily abhishekam to Lord Venkateswara. Sri Ramanuja thought this might hinder other services (kainkaryams) to be introduced: The Vaikhanasa Agama, Ananda Samhita says:

kincha lakshmyabhishEkastu vAsarE bhArgavE harEh sAnnidhya kArI satatam sarva sampatpradAyakah
ಕಿಂಚ ಲಕ್ಷ್ಮ್ಯಭಿಷೇಕಸ್ತು ವಾಸರೇ ಭಾರ್ಗವೇ ಹರೇಃ ಸಾನ್ನಿಧ್ಯ ಕಾರಿ ಸತತಂ ಸರ್ವ ಸಂಪತ್ಪ್ರದಾಯಕಃ
Furthermore, performing Lakshmi abhishekam on Friday brings presence of Hari (Vishnu) & always bestows all kinds of wealth.

On Fridays when the Lord had Tirumanjanam, Sridevi adorning His chest had to be removed for her bath. Since she cannot bear even an instant's separation from her beloved, Lord Ramanuja ordained that Nachiar Tirumoli, a poem of 143 sublime verses sung by Sri Andal (Goda Lakshmi) who was an incarnation of both Sridevi and Bhudevi should be recited on that occasion to miti­gate the pangs of separation. The presence of Lakshmi on the body of the Hill god is responsible for the name Sri-nivasa (the abode of Lakshmi) for Him.

Vakshastala Lakshmi Stotram

Every morning after the usual Thomala Seva and Koluvu, Kesavadi Chaturvimsati (24) Namavali followed by Sri Venkateswara Sahasranama Archana is recited, while tulasi leaves are being offered by the Vaikhanasa Archaka, at the lotus feet of Lord Venkateswara as part of Nitya Tiruvaradhanam. Immediately after the recital of Sri Venkateswara Sahasranama Archana (1008 holy names), the Vakshastala Lakshmi is offered with Chaturvimsati Namavali from Varaha Puranam, while the sacred Tulasi leaves from the lotus feet of Srivaru is being offered to Goddess Lakshmi and Goddess Padmavathi, present at the Chest of Srivaru. This stotram is considered very sacred as it is meant for Vyuha Lakshmi present on the Chest of Lord Venkateswara and it will give lot of wealth and prosperity to the devotees who recite. After every Archana (during I Archana, II Archana and Night Archana) this stotram is offered to Goddess Lakshmi.
